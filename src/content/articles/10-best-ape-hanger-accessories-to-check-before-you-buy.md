Ape hangers are the heartbeat of any true cholo-style bagger build—giving your bike that unmistakable lowrider swagger and commanding presence on the street. Before you pull the trigger on your next set, it’s crucial to check the best ape hanger accessories that can make or break your ride’s comfort, style, and safety.

## Key takeaways
- Proper grips and throttle sleeves improve control and reduce hand fatigue on long cruise nights.
- Switch housings and brake/clutch levers designed for ape hangers ensure ergonomic hand positioning.
- Bar-end mirrors offer functionality that complements the high-rise bars without compromising the look.
- Cable and brake line routing kits are essential for avoiding binding and achieving a clean install.
- Accessory mounting options, including GPS and cupholder adapters, add modern convenience to classic ape hangers.

## Why Choosing the Right Ape Hanger Accessories Matters

I’ve spent years tweaking ape hangers on my own builds and trust me, it’s not just about grabbing the tallest or flashiest bars you can find. Ape hangers dramatically change your riding posture—and that changes everything from how your clutch hand moves to wrist comfort and even braking feel. Picking out high-quality, compatible grips, levers, mirrors, and routing kits tailored to ape hangers turns what could be a painful ride into a smooth, stylish cruise. I’ve tested countless grip textures and lever styles, so I’m sharing what you need to check before you buy that perfect set for your Vicla or lowrider-inspired bagger.

---

## 1. Grips and Throttle Sleeves: Grip Comfort and Control

Your hands are how you command your bike, so grip design matters big time on apes. I recommend looking for grips with thicker rubber or gel inserts to reduce vibration, something that many low-and-slow riders overlook until their hands go numb after a few hours on cruise nights.

- **Pro tip:** Match your grips with a quality throttle sleeve specifically sized for ape hangers. Some sleeves are longer or have smoother rotation, which makes throttle control buttery smooth and less twitchy in tight city stops.
- Popular choices like the **Arlen Ness Rubber Grips** or **Kuryakyn Dual Compound Grips** hit the sweet spot on comfort and style.
- Avoid grips with overly aggressive knurling that can irritate skin through gloves.

---

## 2. Brake and Clutch Levers: Ergonomics for Extended Control

Stock levers often don’t play well with tall bars. Longer levers with adjustable reach will save your hands from cramping, especially if your ape hangers push your arms high and wide. I’ve swapped multiple OEM levers for brands like **Kuryakyn ISO Levers** that feature adjustable pivots.

- Check the lever mount diameter because ape hangers sometimes need different clamp sizes.
- Look for adjustable lever reach so you can dial in just the right pull length for your hands.

---

## 3. Switch Housings: Keeping Controls Within Easy Reach

High ape bars can leave your controls feeling a stretch away—dangerous during quick gear changes or signal presses. Upgraded switch housings relocate buttons and sometimes extend wiring to make operation intuitive and fail-free.

- Brands like **Bikers Choice** or **Kuryakyn** offer ape hanger-specific switch housings designed for a clean setup.
- Consider re-wiring the harness with longer cables during your install to prevent tautness or cable wear.

---

## 4. Bar-End Mirrors: Function Meets Style

Ape hangers and mirrors can be a tricky combo—you want good rear view without messing with the tall bar’s smooth lines. Bar-end mirrors edge out as the top choice here.

- I’ve tested options like **Memphis Shades Bar-End Mirrors**; they provide wide, adjustable views and don’t interfere with hand controls.
- Remember to select left- and right-side specific mirrors designed for the diameter of your ape bar ends.
- Keep an eye on finish options (chrome, matte black) to match your chrome prep or blacked-out setup perfectly.

---

## 5. Cable and Brake Line Routing Kits: Clean Looks and Smooth Operation

The last thing you want with towering ape hangers is a spaghetti mess of cables binding or snagging. Specialized cable kits include stainless steel liners with extended lengths optimized for apes.

- Kits from **Goodridge** or **Kuryakyn** ensure no binding, no kinked cables, and smooth lever return.
- If you’re going hydraulic, check for compatible braided brake lines designed for longer run lengths.
- Spend time routing cables carefully during installation to avoid chafing—this improves longevity.

---

## 6. Accessory Mounts and Convenience Add-Ons

Lowrider-inspired baggers aren’t just about show—they’re for the cruise nights, meetups, and long nights that call for some creature comforts. Don’t overlook accessories like:

- **GPS Mounts:** For those who want directions discreetly mounted near their ape hangers.
- **Cupholder Adapters:** Keeping hydration close without sacrificing style.
- USB chargers or phone docks compatible with ape hanger bars make charging on the go a breeze.

## FAQ

### What height of ape hangers is best for lowrider-inspired baggers?

Most Vicla and lowrider bagger builders stick to ape hangers in the 12 to 16-inch range for that classic cholo look while maintaining control on city streets. Taller bars can look cool but may cause discomfort and complicate wiring.

### Can I install ape hanger accessories myself?

Absolutely. With some basic hand tools and patience, many riders handle grips, levers, and mirrors installations themselves. Be sure to check cable lengths and electrical fits carefully—refer to product specs and consider your bike’s handlebar diameter.

### How do I avoid cable binding with tall ape hangers?

Use cable routing kits designed for ape hangers with longer cables and smooth liners. During install, route cables with gradual bends, not sharp angles, and test lever/fork movement frequently before final tightening.

### Are bar-end mirrors safe on ape hangers?

When mounted securely and selected with proper diameter fit, bar-end mirrors provide excellent rear visibility without interfering with controls. Adjustable mounts ensure you can fine-tune viewing angles for maximum safety.

### What grips offer the best balance of comfort and style?

Brands like Arlen Ness and Kuryakyn produce grips that combine gel inserts for comfort with classic or lowrider aesthetic rubber patterns, making them favorites among builders keeping their Vicla’s signature vibe intact.
